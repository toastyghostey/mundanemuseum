ABOUT US

What is Mundane?

mun·dane

/mənˈdān/

adjective

adjective: mundane

  • lacking interest or excitement; dull.
    • "seeking a way out of his mundane, humdrum existence"
  • of this earthly world rather than a heavenly or spiritual one.
    • "the boundaries of the mundane world"

To us, mundane is far from boring.

The boundaries of the mundane world have been met time and time again, and it's these same boundaries that tie all human experience together to create something truly special.

It is through seeing the lives of those long past that we can truly gain a form of appreciation for life as a form of art itself.

Who Are We?

We are a team of scientists, historians, professors, anthropologists, artists, musicians, left handed people, people who wash their hands, people who think out loud, people who...

... well, we could go on, but in short — we’re you!

We’re a group of people dedicated to preserving the every day lives of people throughout all walks of life in every time. From the stone age to the present, our digital museum is half museum and half art exhibit and statement.

Questions and Answers

Q. Is this a real location? Can I go visit it in real life with my friends and family?

A. Unfortunately not! We are keeping our operating costs low and remaining an online collective so we can focus on bringing history to everyone at a free cost.

Q. How carefully vetted and fact checked is the information on this site?

A. Very much so! We include hyperlinks into all our cited information, and frequently collaborate with real professionals in the field with verified credentials.

Q. Is this a non-profit or for-profit organization?

A. This is a non-profit organization. All of the proceeds go towards keeping the lights on and paying for the time of professionals. The owners do not see a single cent from any purchases or donations.

Q. Is it really all that mundane if the content here is meant to be interesting?

A. Of course the content here is interesting, otherwise no one would read it! However, no one would read it either if they didn’t have the initial question of “what does a mundane museum actually look like?”
